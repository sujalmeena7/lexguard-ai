Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 23, 2026

At LexGuard AI, we take your privacy as seriously as the compliance audits we perform. This policy outlines our commitment to the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023 and how we handle your information.

1. Data Minimization & Ephemeral Processing

LexGuard is engineered to be a non-custodial auditing platform. We do not store the documents you upload for audit. All legal documents processed via our "Live Audit" or "Dashboard" environments are stored in an ephemeral, session-scoped vector store (ChromaDB) and are purged immediately upon the termination of your session.

2. Information We Collect

We only collect the minimum information necessary to provide our services:

  • Contact Information: When you unlock a report or request a pilot, we collect your name, email, and company details to facilitate delivery and business communication.
  • Usage Metadata: We track anonymized audit counts and risk verdict frequencies to maintain our "Live Activity Ticker" and improve our detection algorithms.

3. No Model Training

Crucial Commitment: We never use your uploaded documents, legal clauses, or audit results to train our LLMs (Llama 3) or any third-party AI models. Your proprietary legal data remains yours.

4. Third-Party Processing

We utilize Groq LPU™ for high-speed inference. Data sent to Groq for analysis is processed ephemerally and is not retained for training purposes by the infrastructure provider.

5. Your Rights under DPDP 2023

As a data principal in India, you have the right to access, rectify, and erase your personal data. Since we do not retain your documents, these rights primarily apply to the contact information you provide during lead capture.
